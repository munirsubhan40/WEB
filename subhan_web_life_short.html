My Happy Life Style?

  • Define Your Goals: Start by identifying what success means to you. What are your personal, professional, and life goals? Knowing what you want to achieve is the first step.

                 

  • Create a Plan: Once you've identified your goals, create a roadmap to reach them. Break your long-term goals into smaller, achievable steps. This plan will give you a clear direction.

    •       

  • Education and Self-Improvement: Continue to learn and improve your skills and knowledge. This can be through formal education, workshops, online courses, or self-study. Constantly investing in your personal and professional growth is key.

    •      

  • Work Hard: Success often requires dedication and hard work. Be prepared to put in the effort required to achieve your goals. This may involve long hours and overcoming challenges.

    •      

  • Stay Persistent: Success rarely comes overnight. It often involves setbacks and failures. Be persistent and resilient in the face of obstacles.

    •        

  • Network: Building a strong network can be crucial for success. Connect with people who share your goals or who can help you achieve them. Networking can provide opportunities, advice, and support.

    •       

  • Maintain a Healthy Work-Life Balance: Success should not come at the expense of your health, relationships, or overall well-being. Ensure you maintain a balance in your life.

    •        

  • Manage Your Finances: Financial stability is a component of success for many people. Budgeting, saving, and making wise financial decisions can contribute to a successful life.


    •       

  • Practice Self-Care: Take care of your physical and mental health. Regular exercise, proper nutrition, and mindfulness can help you stay focused and energized.

    •      

  • Adapt and Learn from Failure: Don't be discouraged by failures; instead, see them as opportunities for growth. Learn from your mistakes and adjust your approach accordingly.

    •      

  • Seek Purpose and Happiness: Success is not just about material accomplishments. It's also about finding purpose and happiness in what you do. Pursue your passions and find meaning in your work and personal life.


  • Give Back: Success can be even more meaningful when you contribute to your community or a cause you believe in. Volunteering and helping others can be fulfilling.

MaryBeth Hyland Success story:

                                                                                           MaryBeth Hyland started her company Spark Vision to help businesses create and maintain collaborative and inclusive office cultures. Specializing in millennial engagement, Hyland and Spark Vision offer workshops to help offices foster collaboration and connections among workers. A child of abuse, Hyland has faced a lot of challenges to her self-worth and confidence. She uses her survival daily as a way to motivate her to succeed in business, drawing on the experience of her past to connect with the people she works with. According to her website, Hyland says she thinks that her experience makes her more successful in her company. The takeaway: Everyone has a past—and not all are good. But whatever you’ve gone through, using your background and experience to inform your business can be great, even if your experiences aren’t. In Hyland’s case, she uses a troubled childhood to motivate and drive her, and her business benefits from it.

BIANCA’S SUCCESS STORY:

                                                          When it comes to entrepreneur stories, there are few as powerful as Bianca Lily’s. Bianca learned in high school that there is a predetermined path that people were meant to take: First university, then a lifetime of working diligently for one company until you retire at age 65. Fortunately, Bianca listened to a cassette tape of a man named Tony Robbins and she decided she was going to break the chains of convention and surge onto the battlefield of business and follow her own path. However, even positive shifts are driven by the meanings we ascribe to them. From that day forward Bianca defiantly classified herself as “unemployable” because she “never wanted to do a Monday to Friday, nine-to-five thing.” She cannot even remember the number of jobs she held in her early adult life, always hungry for something that would finally fulfill her ambitions.



FINDING HER WAY:

                                                She became self-employed as a personal trainer, driven by a desire to help people discover how to live healthy lifestyles. But as many great entrepreneur stories go, Bianca soon realized she had not yet discovered her passion and was a slave to her work. She felt trapped once again. On her quest to find fulfillment, Bianca left her job and began traveling the world with her husband, looking for an opportunity that would blend her passion with profitability. After achieving a certain amount of success in network marketing, Bianca attended Business Mastery, seeking to create massive, unparalleled growth within her business. As most entrepreneur stories go, Bianca’s life changed with a rude awakening. “I realized I wasn’t a business owner or operator. I thought I had a business, but I can’t sell it. I don’t own the business that I’m a network marketer for. What I have is a residual income that is going to allow me to do something greater.” Bianca learned that she craved the life of an entrepreneur. She realized that she is always looking for that spark in people – that vision that lights a person up and compels them to take action immediately. It was the entrepreneur’s true gift of constantly scouting talent and cultivating potential that truly brought her joy.
